6.1 Periodic Trends & Properties

Key ideas

  • Periodic Law: Properties of elements recur periodically when arranged by atomic number.
  • Atomic Radius: Increases down a group (more shells) and decreases across a period (stronger nuclear pull).
  • Ionization Energy (IE): Energy to remove an electron. Increases across a period (harder to remove) and decreases down a group (easier to remove due to shielding).
  • Electronegativity (EN): Attraction for shared electrons. Fluorine is the highest (4.0). Noble gases are excluded.

Important formulas & their meaning

  • Effective Nuclear Charge: Z_eff = Z - S.
    Explains why pull increases across a period. Z is protons, S is shielding electrons.

Short example(s)

  • Radius: K > Na > Li (Size increases down).
  • Reactivity: Alkali metals (Group 1) get more reactive down the group (easier to lose e⁻). Halogens (Group 17) get less reactive down the group (harder to gain e⁻).

6.2 Chemical Bonding

Key ideas

  • Ionic Bond: Transfer of electrons from Metal to Non-metal. Held by electrostatic attraction.
  • Covalent Bond: Sharing of electrons between Non-metals.
  • Polar Covalent: Unequal sharing due to electronegativity difference. Creates dipoles (δ+ / δ-).

Important formulas & their meaning

  • Bond Type by ΔEN:
    • 0.0 - 0.4: Non-polar Covalent
    • 0.4 - 1.7: Polar Covalent
    • > 1.7: Ionic

Short example(s)

  • NaCl: ΔEN = |3.0 - 0.9| = 2.1 (Ionic).
  • H-Cl: ΔEN = |3.0 - 2.1| = 0.9 (Polar Covalent).
  • Cl-Cl: ΔEN = 0 (Non-polar Covalent).

6.3 Lewis Structures & Molecular Shape

Key ideas

  • Octet Rule: Atoms bond to achieve 8 valence electrons (Stable noble gas configuration).
  • VSEPR Theory: Valence Shell Electron Pair Repulsion. Electron groups repel to be as far apart as possible, determining geometry.
  • Resonance: When multiple valid structures produce a hybrid average (e.g., Ozone, Benzene).

Important formulas & their meaning

  • AXE Notation: A = Central Atom, X = Bonding Atoms, E = Lone Pairs.
    Determines shape (e.g., AX4 is Tetrahedral, AX2E2 is Bent).

Short example(s)

  • Methane (CH₄): 4 bonds, 0 lone pairs. Tetrahedral (109.5°).
  • Ammonia (NH₃): 3 bonds, 1 lone pair. Trigonal Pyramidal (107°).
  • Water (H₂O): 2 bonds, 2 lone pairs. Bent (104.5°).
