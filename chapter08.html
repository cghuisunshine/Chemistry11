Big Idea

Organic chemistry is the study of carbon-containing compounds. Carbon is unique because it forms four strong covalent bonds, allowing for infinite chains, rings, and complex structures.

Key Concept A single change in structure (isomerism) or functional group creates a completely new substance with different properties.

8.1 Simple Hydrocarbons & Isomerism

Hydrocarbon Families

Family Type Bonds General Formula
Alkane Saturated Single C–C CₙH₂ₙ₊₂
Alkene Unsaturated Double C=C CₙH₂ₙ
Alkyne Unsaturated Triple C≡C CₙH₂ₙ₋₂
Aromatic Unsaturated Benzene Ring (varies)

Naming Rules (IUPAC)

  1. Find the longest continuous carbon chain (Parent).
  2. Number the chain starting from the end closest to a branch (or double/triple bond).
  3. Name branches as alkyl groups (methyl, ethyl, propyl).
  4. List branches alphabetically. Use prefixes (di-, tri-) for duplicates.
Example 3-ethyl-2,3-dimethylhexane
Parent: Hexane (6 carbons)
Branches: Ethyl at C3, Methyls at C2 and C3.

Isomers

Structural Isomers: Same molecular formula, different connectivity.
Geometric Isomers (Cis/Trans): Same connectivity, different shape around a double bond.

8.2 Functional Groups

A functional group is a specific arrangement of atoms that defines the chemical reactivity of a molecule.

Oxygen-Containing Groups

Group Structure Recognition Usage
Alcohol R–OH -ol Fuels, Beverages
Ether R–O–R' ether Solvents
Aldehyde R–CHO C=O at end Preservatives
Ketone R–CO–R' C=O in middle Solvents (Acetone)
Carboxylic Acid R–COOH C=O and -OH Weak Acids (Vinegar)
Ester R–COO–R' C=O and -O-C Fragrances (Fruity)

Nitrogen-Containing Groups

  • Amine: R–NH₂ (Simple nitrogen attachment).
  • Amide: R–CONH₂ (Nitrogen attached to C=O). Found in proteins.

8.3 Reactions of Organic Molecules

1. Combustion

Burning in oxygen. Always produces CO₂, H₂O and energy (if complete).

CH₄ + 2O₂ → CO₂ + 2H₂O

2. Substitution

Replacing an atom (usually H) with another (usually Halogen).

CH₄ + Cl₂ → CH₃Cl + HCl (Requires UV light)

3. Addition

Breaking a double/triple bond to add atoms (Saturated product).

C₂H₄ (ethene) + Cl₂ → C₂H₄Cl₂ (dichloroethane)

4. Elimination

Removing atoms to create a double bond (Unsaturated product).

C₂H₅OH (ethanol) → C₂H₄ (ethene) + H₂O

5. Esterification

Acid + Alcohol → Ester + Water.

Critical Reaction Ethanoic Acid + Ethanol → Ethyl Ethanoate + Water

6. Polymerization

  • Addition Polymer: Monomers with double bonds join (e.g., Polyethene).
  • Condensation Polymer: Monomers join by releasing small molecules like water (e.g., Nylon, Polyester).

Master Summary

Organic chemistry is about patterns. Recognize the Functional Group → Predict the Property. Recognize the Bond Type (Single vs Double) → Predict the Reaction (Substitution vs Addition).
